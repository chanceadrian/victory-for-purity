InDecision

Big decisions rarely come with clear answers. That’s why InDecision helps you find clarity by turning gut feelings into something visible, audible—even recordable. It presents you with options to react to, criteria to start with, and an open space to discover what matters most.

Options & Criteria

Think aloud.

You already know what matters. You just haven’t heard yourself say it yet. InDecision helps you get there by presenting contrasting options designed to surface your intuition, your values, and the tradeoffs you didn’t know you were making.

By listening to how you respond to options, InDecision turns your reactions into structured, editable criteria that you can tap to edit and weigh. In your first round, keep up to 2 must-haves, 3 should-haves, and as many nice-to-haves as you like.

To stress-test your priorities, toggle criteria on.

What, if, would — and how. Sharpen your thinking with “what if” and “would you” questions. InDecision then suggests updates that prompt deeper reflection—uncovering priorities, refining definitions, and helping you get crystal clear about what matters.

Decision ID

Save progress.

Every decision ends with a Decision ID. It's a snapshot of what matters to you in a particular decision and why. Use them to help jumpstart a new one with built-in context. Or use a friend’s to co-navigate something big, like moving in together or planning for retirement.

Back to the drawing board. You can always return to earlier parts of your decision: revisit your options, reshape your criteria, or stress-test what matters most.

What else could you be deciding? InDecision can suggest related choices by remixing pieces of your own thinking across decisions.

Start with just a conversation. Every decision starts with just two simple questions: What decision are you facing? and What matters to you in that decision?

Shared with You

Some decisions aren't
yours alone to make.

Hear your partner’s or friend’s reactions as you explore options together. See which criteria they’ve added, leave notes to explain your thinking, and edit priorities side-by-side as you both discover what’s important to each other.
