Introducing Shelf

The objects of your life.
Shelved beautifully.

Shelf displays your favorite things in a way that is more thoughtful. More beautiful. And ultimately more personal. Its a remarkably simple display case for photos, notes, links, and more one built on a profound belief: that the way we organize our lives shapes how we experience them.

Shelf Artifacts

Frame something wonderful.

Storytelling through what we keep. Shelf Artifacts are simple, expressive containers for things worth revisiting. Each one holds a piece of life as its happening a passing thought, a favorite scene, a love note you dont want to lose. And with Time Capsules, you can lock any item until the moment feels right. Times the only password youll need.

Add, Style, & Archive

There's a
cabinet for that.

Make it yours. Tucked at the bottom of the shelf, the cabinet is how you shape everything that happens above it. Its where you add new artifacts, find the right shade with styling tools, and archive items.

Your eye, with a little help. Ask Siri for what you have in mind a weekend in California or photos of the kids when they were little and Shelf will show you photos that best complement your space.

iPad & Mac

Shelf love.

Huge in almost every way. On iPad, Shelf can fit up to three visible spaces at once, making it easier to see, sort, and shape your world with room to spare. Drag between Spaces or open Rooms side by side for a wider view of your collection.

From anywhere to here. Drag a note, photo, or link from another app and Shelf transforms it into a personal artifact, styled to match your space.

Slideover, iPadOS

Made to move between things. On Mac, each artifact becomes its own window so you can write, reference, and rearrange across your Shelf with the ease of a desktop.

Apple Vision Pro

Designed to be loved,
shaken, or thrown.

There's emotional interaction. Inspired by how we handle objects in the real world, Shelf responds to physical gestures. Shake a shelf to clear to clear it, or throw an item to send it to the archive.

Rebuild your shelf, item by item. Reshelf is for starting over a way to take inventory and decide, one item at a time, what to keep and what to let go.

Apple Watch & Friends

All shelves hold things.

This one makes the most
of them.

We all keep notes and photos. But what happens around them is different for everyone. Working with your other devices, Shelf evaluates how factors like your heart rate or environment might influence which items you revisit often and why.

Tap the bicycle on iPhone to view summaries for any Space or Room.

A matter of time and place. Shelf appears in your Smart Stack with notes grouped by shared context, like where you were or how it felt outside.

Smart Stack, watchOS

Stay closer to what you care about. Shelf on Apple Watch opens with a list of items quotes from your own work, frequented revisited artifacts, or photos you havent seen in a while.

Choose between Most Visited, Reflect, or Favorites three distinct collections, all from your wrist.

Share with Friends

You've got mail.

Better than a Hallmark card. Send a note, photo, or even a full room. Standard delivery sends sometime between now and a few days from now. Or choose Scheduled for birthdays or an anniversary.

Items sent in Messages appear in Shared with You.

Apple TV

Introspection hits home.

Reflect from across the room. Shelf on the big screen is quiet by design. Browse old photos and favorites, with space to read their original captions. Use Siri remote to browse and filter, or simply let Shelf play in the background.

1 Year Solo Design Exploration
Shelf began with a simple question: Why does any corner of your home feel more personal than your photo library? What followed was a year reimagining how software could feel not as a tool that stores your life, but as a place that honors it.

    Designed in the Great State of Texas. Revised in Pennsylvania. Prototyped in California.

    Shelf concludes my inaugural design contribution. It served as a vessel to instantiate and push the limits of metaphor, self-expression, and personal computing seen starting with the graphical user interface in the original Macintosh.

    Introducing Shelf

    It's utility is purely
    psychological.

    People have always needed to see themselves reflected in their environment. There's just a fundamental human need to curate the spaces around us. To create a visual narrative of who we are. The photograph, the book, the small object from a far place they're markers. Reminders of the people, places, and times that have shaped us.

    What's remarkable is how this process of selection and arrangement helps us understand ourselves more clearly. The act of choosing what to display, where to place it, how it relates to everything else. This becomes a form of self- reflection, expression, definition.

    Because so many of these meaningful objects now exist digitally our favorite quotes, love notes, the photos we revisit again and again the challenge becomes creating something that honors this psychological need, that understands these objects don't just occupy space: they help define the very essence of home.
